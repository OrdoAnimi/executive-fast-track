## Cool notes

### What this really means

Security is not just a control catalogue. At executive level, security is exposure visibility, risk ownership, decision evidence, funding priority, control maturity, and confidence that the organisation can respond when something fails.

### Why executives care

A CISO or CRO is not only asking whether a tool exists. They are asking whether risk is visible, prioritised, owned, funded, evidenced, and explainable to the board before and after an incident.

### Words to steal

- Exposure.
- Risk visibility.
- Control maturity.
- Residual risk.
- Assurance.
- Incident readiness.
- Board reporting.

### Words to avoid

- Security tooling first.
- Compliance checkbox.
- We are secure.
- Best-practice control set without risk context.
- Technical diagram before exposure statement.

### How to say it plainly

"The issue is not whether we have security activity. The issue is whether leadership can see the exposure, understand the residual risk, fund the right controls, and defend the decision if an incident occurs."

### One boardroom sentence

"Security becomes executive language when exposure, ownership, evidence, and residual risk are clear."

## Feynman replay

Explain cyber risk to a CFO and Chair without mentioning specific tools. Focus on exposure, money, evidence, and consequence.

## Scenario application

Take one system, product, or AI initiative and write the executive risk frame: exposure, controls, residual risk, owner, evidence, and decision needed.
