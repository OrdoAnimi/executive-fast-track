## Cool notes

### What this really means

The market does not buy the internal structure of your capability. It buys a promise it understands, a pain it recognises, a credible reason to act, and enough trust to believe the outcome can be delivered.

### Why executives care

CMOs and growth executives care about attention, differentiation, trust, and conversion. If the message cannot travel, the capability will not sell. If the audience cannot recognise the pain, the product sounds clever but irrelevant.

### Words to steal

- Market narrative.
- Differentiation.
- Category.
- Demand.
- Trust.
- Conversion.
- Buyer relevance.

### Words to avoid

- We built a framework.
- It has many modules.
- Comprehensive capability.
- Technical superiority without buyer pain.
- Internal language as external positioning.

### How to say it plainly

"The market does not care that the system is elegant. It cares whether the message names a problem buyers already feel, shows a credible path to relief, and gives them a reason to trust us now."

### One boardroom sentence

"If the market cannot repeat the value in its own language, the product is not positioned yet."

## Feynman replay

Explain your product to a buyer who does not care about your framework. Start with the pain, not the mechanism.
