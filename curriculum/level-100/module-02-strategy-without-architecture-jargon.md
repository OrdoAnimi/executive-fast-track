## Cool notes

### What this really means

Strategy fails when an organisation cannot turn intent into coordinated execution. The executive conversation is not "what framework should we use?" It is "where is value leaking between strategy, decision, ownership, funding, and delivery?"

### Why executives care

Executives are judged by movement. A strategy that does not convert into execution becomes reputational and financial drag. Your role is to expose the conversion gap and show how to close it.

### Words to steal

- Strategy-to-execution gap.
- Value leakage.
- Operating model.
- Decision velocity.
- Accountable ownership.
- Performance rhythm.

### Words to avoid

- Capability map first.
- Reference architecture first.
- Artefact maturity.
- Governance forum design before the decision is clear.

### How to say it plainly

"The strategy is not the weak point yet. The weak point is the conversion layer: decision ownership, operating rhythm, funding sequence, and the evidence needed to move from intent to performance."

### One boardroom sentence

"You do not have a strategy problem yet; you have a strategy-to-execution conversion problem."

## Feynman replay

Explain strategy execution as if the CEO asked: "Why are we not moving faster?" Answer without saying architecture.

## Optional Copilot drill

Tell the Copilot: "A CEO says we need to go to market with velocity. Make me answer in strategy execution language, not architecture language."

## Scenario application

Take one product, project, or consulting offer and identify where value could leak between strategy, decision, funding, delivery, and market outcome.
