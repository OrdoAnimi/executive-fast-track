# Content Standards

## Course unit structure

Every Executive Fast Track module should follow the same learning container.

Required fields:

- Module title.
- Level.
- Executive outcome.
- Video anchor.
- Transcript placeholder.
- PDF notes placeholder.
- Optional reading.
- Cool notes.
- Executive vocabulary.
- Terms to avoid.
- One boardroom sentence.
- Optional Feynman replay.
- Optional Copilot drill.
- Scenario application.

## Writing style

Use concise executive prose. Avoid academic padding, generic leadership slogans, excessive theory, and consultant theatre.

Every lesson must help the learner say something useful in a real executive room.

## Cool notes standard

Cool notes are the practical distillation after a video, transcript, or reading.

They should answer:

- What this really means.
- Why executives care.
- What language matters.
- What not to say.
- How to apply it.
- The one sentence to remember.

## Executive translation standard

Every concept must be translated into business language. The translation should cover:

- Decision.
- Value.
- Risk.
- Cost.
- Timing.
- Accountability.
- Evidence.
- Recommended action.

## Resource curation standard

External resources should be used as learning anchors, not copied. The course may link to public videos, public articles, open course pages, and official institutional material. It must not reproduce proprietary course content, paid course material, or copyrighted transcripts without permission.

## Module completion

A module can be completed through low-energy, study, or performance mode. Completion should not require a quiz unless the learner chooses assessment mode.
