## Cool notes

### What this really means

The board does not need the operational story first. It needs the decision, options, trade-off, risk, assurance, accountability, and consequence. Boardroom awareness means understanding what the room is legally, strategically, and reputationally exposed to.

### Why executives care

A board paper or boardroom recommendation that buries the decision inside background material wastes the room's attention and weakens confidence. The board needs enough information to govern, not every operational detail.

### Words to steal

- Oversight.
- Duty.
- Assurance.
- Options considered.
- Recommended course.
- Residual risk.
- Decision record.

### Words to avoid

- Deep dive before the decision.
- Technical walkthrough.
- We believe without evidence.
- Governance as meeting cadence.
- Detail as a substitute for confidence.

### How to say it plainly

"For the board, the issue is not how the mechanism works. The issue is whether management understands the options, the trade-off, the risk being accepted, and the evidence behind the recommendation."

### One boardroom sentence

"The board does not need every detail; it needs enough clarity to govern the decision."

## Feynman replay

Explain the same proposal once to a delivery team and once to a board. Notice what changes.

Take one existing project and convert it into a boardroom frame: decision required, options, recommendation, risk, evidence, next governance checkpoint.
