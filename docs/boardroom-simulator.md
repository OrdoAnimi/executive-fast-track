# Boardroom Simulator

## Purpose

The Boardroom Simulator is the Level 300 capstone environment. It trains the learner to present an idea, proposal, product, transformation, or recommendation to a simulated executive room and respond under pressure.

The simulator is not a pitch deck generator. It is a pressure-testing environment for executive language, decision clarity, commercial value, risk ownership, and evidence.

## Scenario intake

The learner provides:

- Proposal or idea.
- Target organisation or context.
- Desired decision.
- Executive audience.
- Known risks.
- Funding or resource implication.
- Time pressure.
- Current objection or resistance.

## Room composition

Default simulated room:

- CEO.
- CFO.
- CIO.
- COO.
- CMO.
- CISO or CRO.
- Chair.

Optional roles:

- Investor.
- Procurement lead.
- Transformation sponsor.
- General counsel.
- CHRO.
- Business unit executive.

## Simulation flow

1. Define the decision being requested.
2. Clarify the commercial value.
3. Identify the risk and trade-off.
4. Frame the recommendation.
5. Run executive objections.
6. Rewrite weak answers.
7. Produce a boardroom-ready one-pager.
8. Capture the decision memo.

## Objection types

### CEO

- Why now?
- What changes commercially?
- What is the strategic risk if we do nothing?
- Can the organisation execute this?

### CFO

- What does it cost?
- What is the return?
- What waste does this remove?
- How do we control spend?

### CIO

- Can this integrate?
- What delivery risk exists?
- What does this do to technical debt?
- Does this preserve enterprise control?

### COO

- How does this change the operating rhythm?
- Who owns the work?
- What breaks at scale?
- How does this become repeatable?

### CMO

- Why will the market care?
- How does this differentiate us?
- What audience does this serve?
- How does it convert to demand, trust, or adoption?

### CISO/CRO

- What exposure are we reducing?
- What evidence do we have?
- Who owns residual risk?
- What happens if this fails?

### Chair

- What decision are you asking us to make?
- What options were considered?
- What trade-off are we accepting?
- What assurance do we have?

## Output artefacts

- Executive one-pager.
- Objection map.
- Decision memo.
- Role-specific talking points.
- Boardroom sentence bank.
- Next-action recommendation.

## Quality bar

A successful simulation produces a recommendation that is clear, commercially serious, risk-aware, concise, and specific about the decision required.
